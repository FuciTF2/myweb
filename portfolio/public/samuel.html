§ ASSESSMENT OVERVIEW

Samuel Briggs is not a soldier.

Not a survivor.

Not a victim.

He is the architect.

§ PRIOR IDENTITY — ENZO BRIGESCI CLASSIFIED

Before he was SAMUEL BRIGGS, he was ENZO BRIGESCI. A chemist. A prodigy. A man whose work consistently pushed beyond what professional consensus considered safe, ethical, or permissible.

Then — an accident.

INCIDENT — BRIGESCI, E. — ████████████

Experimental compound release. Uncontrolled dispersal. Documented outcomes include widespread nerve failure across a civilian population — the scale of which rendered containment impossible and accountability unavoidable.


Affected individuals: ████████. Long-term neurological damage: confirmed permanent. Responsible party: BRIGESCI, E. — subsequently declared missing.

That should have ended him. Instead, ENZO BRIGESCI ceased to exist — and SAMUEL BRIGGS appeared. Not in hiding. Not in fear. In pursuit of continuation.

§ PSYCHOLOGICAL CORE — THE BELIEF CRITICAL ASSESSMENT

Understanding BRIGGS requires understanding a single foundational premise that underlies every decision he has made:

He does not see the accident as a failure. He sees it as proof that his ideas worked — just not yet in a controlled form.

Everything that followed is an attempt to achieve that control.

He does not hate people. He does not view them with contempt in the conventional sense. His view is more clinical than that, and therefore considerably more dangerous:

FEAR Limits action. An evolutionary remnant with no positive function in an optimal operative. Correctable.
EMOTION Clouds judgement. Introduces unpredictability. A design flaw, not a feature. Suppressible.
MORALITY Restricts progress. A social construct mistaken for an absolute. In BRIGGS's framework, entirely irrelevant.

These are not, in his view, human traits to be preserved. They are inefficiencies to be engineered out. Like any flaw — they can be corrected.

§ THE BERKSLEY UNIT — HIS MASTERPIECE

The BERKSLEY program is not, in BRIGGS's conception, an experiment. It is an attempt to fix humanity at its core.

CHEMISTRY Fear response eliminated via compound regimen developed over years of prior research. BERKSLEY was not the first attempt. It was the first controlled one.
PHARMACOLOGY Performance enhancement beyond natural human limits. Sustained over extended periods. Long-term effects: considered acceptable data.
SELECTION Children chosen specifically. Not out of cruelty — out of practicality. Younger subjects are more malleable. Easier to shape. Easier to refine. BRIGGS noted this in writing.

The casualty rate was not a problem to be minimised. It was a data point to be recorded.

§ THE GOD COMPLEX — HIDDEN IN PLAIN SIGHT ANALYST WARNING

Analysts who encounter BRIGGS for the first time consistently underestimate him. This is by design.

He does not present as unstable. He presents as:

CALM Measured. Unhurried. Gives the impression of a man with nothing to prove. He believes this because it is true — in his own framework.
PROFESSIONAL Refers to subjects by designation. Discusses outcomes in clinical language. Maintains emotional distance that reads as objectivity.
CONVINCING The most dangerous quality. He doesn't believe he's playing god. He believes he's doing what others are too weak to do. This conviction is total. It reads as sanity.

That is what makes him terrifying. Not the certainty of a fanatic. The certainty of a man who has never once needed to question himself.

§ VIEW OF UNIT MEMBERS — RESULTS, NOT PEOPLE

BRIGGS does not see BERKSLEY survivors as people. He sees them as results. Each one tells him something. Each one validates or challenges his work.

BRK-01 BROOK, W.D. ASSESSMENT Proof of resilience. The most complete output of the program. Still functioning after everything. Proof that it worked.
BRK-02 DUKKEBRIE, H. ASSESSMENT Uncontrolled variable. Functional but chaotic. Useful data on suppression limits. Outcome noted without sentiment.
BRK-03 DAVID, F. ASSESSMENT Optimal function. Closest to intended output. Precision and composure combined. The benchmark.
BRK-04 KAPLERI, A. ASSESSMENT Intellectual deviation. Retained too much. Interesting anomaly. Death provided data on what happens when the human element persists.
§ THE BETRAYAL — A PROCEDURAL NOTE KEY EVENT

When BRIGGS releases BROOK's hand, it is not an emotional act. Analysts who frame it as betrayal are importing a framework BRIGGS does not share.

In his view:

THE MISSION Complete. All objectives achieved. Unit performed as designed across full operational lifespan.
THE UNIT Has served its purpose. Continuation beyond this point introduces unnecessary variables.
BROOK, W.D. Is not being betrayed. Is being concluded. The distinction is not semantic to BRIGGS. It is fundamental.

"This was your final mission. Thank you for your service, William."

— BRIGGS, S. — recorded final communication, termination event

He let go. Filed it. Moved on. That is the most unsettling part. Not that he did it. That it cost him nothing.

CASE CLOSED
§ POST-BERKSLEY — THE MAN WHO WALKS AWAY

BRIGGS does not collapse with the unit. Does not disappear. Does not show signs of guilt, remorse, or the kind of psychological deterioration that follows most people who have done what he has done.

He moves on. New work. New goals. Possibly new experiments. Because in his framework — nothing has failed. Everything has been data.

He is, by the time BROOK resurfaces, operating as though BERKSLEY was a successful chapter that simply ended. It was. By his metrics.

§ FINAL CONFRONTATION — CREATOR AND CREATION

When BROOK finally reaches him, this is not simply revenge. It is a confrontation between two opposing truths that cannot coexist.

BRIGGS believes — he improved humanity. Removed its weaknesses. Made something better.
BROOK is — the consequence of that belief. Walking. Still moving. Coming to finish it.

BRIGGS will not see BROOK as a victim standing in front of him. He will see him as a success.

Even then. Even at the end. That never changes. It is the last and most complete expression of who he is.
